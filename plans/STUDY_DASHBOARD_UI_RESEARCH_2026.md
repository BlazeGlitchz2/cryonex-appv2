# Study Dashboard UI Research

Date: 2026-03-16

## Scope

Reviewed current official product surfaces and messaging from AI-assisted study tools and adjacent study products to identify UI patterns worth translating into Cryonex's Study Dashboard.

## Products Reviewed

- [StudyFetch](https://www.studyfetch.com/)
- [Knowt](https://knowt.com/)
- [NotebookLM](https://notebooklm.google/)
- [Khanmigo / Khan Labs](https://www.khanacademy.org/khan-labs)
- [StudySmarter](https://www.studysmarter.co.uk/)
- [Quizlet Study Guides](https://quizlet.com/features/study-guides)

## High-Signal UI Patterns

1. Source-first beats feature-first.
StudyFetch, Knowt, and NotebookLM all center the source material or notebook before the AI modes. The best products make users feel like they are continuing work on a source, not picking from a random tray of AI tricks.

2. The next action is obvious.
Knowt and Khanmigo keep the path simple: continue, review, or ask. Their interfaces reduce decision fatigue by making one or two actions feel primary and everything else supportive.

3. Modes are organized by study intent, not by model capability.
The strongest flows separate review, testing, focus, capture, and tutoring as study jobs. They do not market the UI around AI itself once the user is inside the product.

4. Progress belongs in a rail or compact signal strip.
Good study UIs surface streaks, queues, and mastery, but they do not let those metrics dominate the main work area. The work surface stays reserved for the actual learning loop.

5. Calm product surfaces read better than heavy AI-glass aesthetics.
The products that feel most credible use restrained contrast, fewer decorative effects, stronger typography, and more deliberate sectioning. Accent color is used to signal importance, not to decorate every card.

## Applied To Cryonex

- Reframed the dashboard as a study workbench rather than a feature gallery.
- Moved desktop stats into a right rail so the main canvas can stay source-first.
- Turned recent materials into a source shelf with a featured continuation card.
- Reworked AI modes into clearer effort lanes instead of a generic card grid.
- Tightened hero copy so it talks about the next useful move, not vague AI productivity language.
- Reduced the overall "glassy" feel in favor of calmer, more product-like surfaces.

## Notes

This research is directional rather than exhaustive. The goal was to pull the most repeatable UI patterns from current product leaders and translate them into Cryonex, not to clone any single interface.
